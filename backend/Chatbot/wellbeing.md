## What this is

This guide offers practical, student-friendly steps related to **Managing Exam Anxiety**. It is designed for everyday academic challenges and aims to support wellbeing during your educational journey. The advice is general and should be adapted to your personal situation.

## Practical Steps

1. Break your revision into small topics and schedule them across days (not all in one night).
2. Use a simple breathing exercise for 2 minutes before starting an exam.
3. Arrive early and prepare your materials to avoid last-minute stress.
4. After each practice test, review mistakes and write a short “fix list”.
5. Practice using past quizzes/exams under timed conditions.

## When to get extra help

Seek additional support if the issue lasts for weeks, affects sleep or daily functioning, or causes you to consider quitting courses. You can contact your academic advisor, student affairs, or counseling services (if available). If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services or a trusted person right away.

## Safety note

- This is supportive guidance, not medical or psychological diagnosis.
- If you feel in danger or might harm yourself, seek immediate help from local emergency services or a trusted person.

## What this is

This guide offers practical, student-friendly steps related to **Dealing with Procrastination**. It is designed for everyday academic challenges and aims to support wellbeing during your educational journey. The advice is general and should be adapted to your personal situation.

## Practical Steps

1. At the end of the day, plan tomorrow’s first task.
2. Remove one distraction (mute notifications, keep phone away).
3. Start with the smallest next action (e.g., open the file, write the first heading).
4. Use a 25-minute focus block, then a 5-minute break (repeat 3–4 times).
5. Pick 3 priorities for today (not 10).

## When to get extra help

Seek additional support if the issue lasts for weeks, affects sleep or daily functioning, or causes you to consider quitting courses. You can contact your academic advisor, student affairs, or counseling services (if available). If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services or a trusted person right away.
